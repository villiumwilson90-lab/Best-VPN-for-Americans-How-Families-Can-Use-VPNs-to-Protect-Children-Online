<h2><strong>What a VPN Can and Cannot Do for Family Safety</strong></h2>
<p>Let us start with honesty: a VPN is not a content filter or parental control. It does not by default block access to inappropriate websites or limit screen time. What it does is:</p>
<ul>
<li>Encrypt your family's internet traffic to protect it from outside observation</li>
<li>Mask your IP address from trackers and advertisers</li>
<li>Protect your children when they use devices on public Wi-Fi</li>
<li>Prevent your ISP from building a profile of your family's browsing habits</li>
</ul>
<p>For actual content filtering and screen time management, you need dedicated parental control tools either on the device level or through your router's settings. These are complementary to, not replacements for, each other.</p>
